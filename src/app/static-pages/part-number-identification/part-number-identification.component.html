Part Number Identification

Here's a guide to help you identify part numbers and ensure the accuracy of your orders.

  1. Dell part numbers usually consist of at least 5 alphanumeric characters. This unique identifier aids in ensuring the authenticity and compatibility of the part.

    2. Example: 3RRN8, N2048P, and 407-BBWL

  2. HP part numbers typically contains 6 numeric digits followed by three alphanumeric digits, like so: 820178-001, 498152-001, and 823371-L21

    3. Suffixes such as N for ROHS Compliant Parts or revisions, and RB for refurbished products, can also appear at the end of part numbers.

    Example: A7214BN and J4135A-RB

  3. IBM has a distinct method for identifying and marking parts, making it simpler for technicians, engineers, and customers to make sure they have the correct part for their systems. Their signature style involves a code composed of seven characters, where the third character is always an alphabetical letter.

    4. Some typical examples include: 13M7156, 00AM111, and 13N0703.

  4. Toshiba has its own conventions for labeling and identifying components. These unique identifiers are essential for ensuring compatibility and maintaining the efficiency of systems. Most Toshiba parts adhere to a 10-character format, starting with a letter. The initiating letter can be one among P, V, F, H, or A.

Examples include: P000290550, V000010720, F000000672, A000000560 and H000075280
