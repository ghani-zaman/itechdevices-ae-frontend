Product Certifications

Product certification ensures that a particular product has successfully passed rigorous performance and quality assurance tests and meets the requirements stated in agreements, rules, or specifications, which are frequently referred to as "certification schemes" in the product certification sector.

Products available on itechdevices.ae may follow manufacturer offered certifications as listed below:

ENERGY STAR This international standard for energy-efficient products started in the USA in 1992 through a joint effort by the Environmental Protection Agency and the Department of Energy. Adopted by several countries, products with the ENERGY STAR label typically consume 20-30% less energy compared to the federal standards. This standard aims to reduce greenhouse gas emissions and other pollutants caused by inefficient energy use, ensuring both cost savings and environmental safety.

RoHS Short for "Restriction of Hazardous Substances Directive," RoHS began in the European Union in 2003. The directive mandates the restriction of specific hazardous materials in electronic and electrical devices. It aims to address the environmental issues related to toxic e-waste and enhance the recyclability of electronic goods. This initiative ensures products are free from harmful substances like lead, mercury, and cadmium.

ISO 14000 This family of standards revolves around environmental management, helping organizations minimize negative environmental impacts. ISO 14000 covers aspects ranging from the complete life cycle of a product to its systematic, process-driven approach for environmental policy. It's not just about environmental compliance; it's about achieving overall environmental excellence.

FCC Certification Established by the Federal Communications Commission, the FCC label certifies that electronic products' electromagnetic interference is within the approved limits. This ensures the safe operation of devices without causing undue interference to other electronic products.

CEC The China Environmental United Certification Center Co., Ltd (CEC) is a prominent figure in China's push towards environmental sustainability. Affiliated with the Ministry of Environment Protection, the CEC guides businesses in adopting greener practices and facilitates negotiations for eco-friendly projects.

VCCI The Voluntary Control Council for Interference ensures that IT equipment in Japan doesn't cause undue electromagnetic interference. Formed in 1985, VCCI-certified products maintain radio frequency emissions within the accepted standards, preventing potential interference with other electronic devices.

CCC The China Compulsory Certificate mark is a critical safety mark for products in the Chinese market. Established in 2003, it amalgamates two previous certification systems, ensuring that a wide range of products meets safety and quality standards before entering the Chinese market.

KC Mark The Korea Certification emphasizes both local and foreign manufacturers' responsibility to ensure the safety of their products. It subjects products to meticulous safety certification processes, ensuring they don't harm people, property, or the environment.

CB Scheme Managed by the International Commission on the Rules for the Approval of Electrical Equipment (IECEE), the CB Scheme facilitates international certification of electrical and electronic products. It's a testament to the global acceptance of a product's safety and performance standards.

TÜV Originating from Germany, TÜVs are independent organizations that validate a wide range of products for safety. From motor vehicles to electronics, they ensure these products adhere to stringent safety criteria, thereby protecting humans and the environment.

UL Certification Founded in 1894, UL (Underwriters Laboratories) has been at the forefront of safety analysis for various technologies. Products that achieve UL certification have been rigorously tested and verified for safety, ensuring they meet defined benchmarks and standards.

CSA Certification CSA International, part of the Canadian Standards Association group, offers product testing and certification services. With its mark recognized globally, the CSA ensures that products adhere to Canadian, U.S., or international standards, guaranteeing quality and safety for consumers.
